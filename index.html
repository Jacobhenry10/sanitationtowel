Sanitation Towels (Sanitary Pads)

1. Introduction

A sanitation towel, also known as a sanitary towel or sanitary pad, is a hygiene product used to absorb menstrual blood. It is worn inside the underwear during menstruation.

2. Types of Sanitation Towels

2.1 Disposable Sanitary Pads

  • Made for single use.
  • Usually include adhesive backing.
  • Come in various sizes.
  • Often packed individually.

2.2 Reusable Cloth Pads

  • Made from fabric materials.
  • Can be washed and reused.
  • Attached with buttons or snaps.
  • Lower environmental impact.

3. Main Components

3.1 Top Sheet

Touches the skin. Made from soft cotton or synthetic fiber.

3.2 Absorbent Core

Absorbs and holds blood. Made of pulp or superabsorbent polymers.

3.3 Back Sheet

Prevents leakage. Made from plastic or waterproof materials.

3.4 Adhesive Strip

Fixes the pad in place. Some pads include side wings for extra grip.

4. Sizes and Absorbency

  • Panty Liners – light flow.
  • Regular Pads – moderate flow.
  • Heavy Flow Pads – high absorbency.
  • Overnight Pads – long and thick for night use.

5. Usage Instructions

  • Wash hands.
  • Remove pad from wrapper.
  • Peel adhesive strip.
  • Place pad inside underwear.
  • Replace every 4–6 hours.
  • Wrap used pad and throw in a trash bin.
  • For cloth pads: rinse, wash, and sun-dry.

6. Hygiene and Health Tips

  • Change pads often.
  • Avoid scented pads if sensitive.
  • Store in dry, clean conditions.
  • Use breathable fabric for comfort.

7. Environmental Impact

  • Disposable pads cause long-term waste.
  • Cloth pads are eco-friendly.
  • Some brands now offer biodegradable options.

8. Availability

  • Found in stores and online.
  • Some areas provide pads for free in schools or clinics.
  • Prices and access vary by location.
